Projects

McMaster Dare

Currently, I am on the McMaster Deep Space Analogue Research Expedition (DARE) Team, working on the electrical subteam. As a first-year student, I have been primarily involved in research with embedded systems, C++ coding for ESP32, Solidworks basics, and schematic/electrical knowledge. Our team focuses on building custom devices for expeditions to explore terrains such as caves and the deep sea, simulating conditions on Mars for future astronauts. I am currently working on Project BELUGA, an attachable device for submarines to explore deep-water trenches. For that, I am designing the PCB. I hope through utilizing opportunities like DARE and DeltaHacks I will equip myself with skills for the future.

Phytoremediation of Crude Oil

A 3-year project built upon a science fair project in grade 8 on how to combat oil spills. In this project, I found valuable research to how to dispose of collected oil after an oil spill using plant enhancers along with specific plants. - Worked at the Trent University Science Emery Laboratory and worked along with Prof. Anna Kisiala. - Got multiple silver medals and ribbons at the Canada Wide Science Fair and presented it to investors at Yorks Lassonde School of Business.
